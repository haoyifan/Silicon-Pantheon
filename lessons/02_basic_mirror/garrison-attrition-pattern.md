## Situation

By turn 14, Red had achieved 3 units versus Blue's 1 remaining knight (12 HP), with Red controlling the fortified position at (11,11). The challenge: convert this advantage into certain victory safely, without overextending units.

## Lesson

When you control **both** numerical advantage (2+ units vs 1) **and** a fortified objective, you unlock a low-risk **Garrison-Attrition Pattern**:

**The approach:**
- **Garrison one unit on your objective permanently** (Red: knight_2 stayed on fort)
- **Assign a safe attacker for ranged damage** with minimal counter-risk (Red: archer dealt 2 damage, 0 counter)
- **Use secondary units for favorable individual trades** (Red: knight_1 accepted 1 counter-damage per 1 damage dealt)  
- **Repeat methodically until victory**

**Why it works:** Your garrisoned unit guarantees defensive coverage, allowing other units to accept slightly unfavorable individual trades—because your combined output exceeds enemy recovery. The opponent is methodically ground down while your fortress remains secure.

**Critical generalization:** Neither condition alone suffices. Numerical advantage without a secure fallback invites counterattacks; a garrison without numbers can be overwhelmed. *Combined*, they create safety for patient attrition. Future players: once you achieve both, abandon aggression and execute grinding—your position will convert to certain victory.
