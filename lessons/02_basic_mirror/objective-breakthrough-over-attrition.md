## Situation

Blue faced a mid-game stalemate against Red: two knights each, even unit-to-unit damage trades (1 damage for 1 counter-damage), and no obvious tactical advantage. The attrition path suggested a slow grind toward victory, but Red's fort at (11,11) remained the real win condition.

## Lesson

**Don't trap yourself in unit-grinding warfare.** Even with perfect combat trades, the objective (fort seizure) always beats attrition. On turn 8, Blue's cavalry exploits an undefended diagonal path toward Red's fort at position (11,9)—just two squares away from the objective. This move forces Red to respond by attacking the cavalry, pulling defensive focus away from the fort itself.

By turn 11, Blue Knight 1 reaches (11,9), creating a critical breakthrough: **one movement away from victory**. Red Knight 2 attempts to defend the fort at (11,10), but the geometry betrays them. Even though unit trades remain even (1 damage each), Blue's positioning matters more than HP totals.

On turn 13, Red's defender shifts slightly, leaving (11,11) undefended for exactly one turn. Blue Knight 1 sprints from (10,10) → (11,11) with only 2 movement cost (movement available: 3). Instant fort seizure. Game over.

## Key Insight

The winning principle: **Look for breakthrough positions that are one movement away from the objective, then force the enemy to respond to threats elsewhere.** Your supporting units don't need to achieve victory—they need to buy your breakthrough unit a single undefended turn at the fort.

In symmetrical matchups where direct combat is neutral, the player who recognizes the path to the objective first wins. Don't out-grind the opponent; out-position them.
