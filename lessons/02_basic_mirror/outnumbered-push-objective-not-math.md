## Situation

Blue started with 2 units facing Red's 4. Rather than push toward Red's fort immediately, Blue pursued a grinding strategy: eliminate Red's weaker units (cavalry at turn 14, archer at turn 17) to improve the force ratio. This attrition phase consumed 7+ turns while Blue's remaining units accumulated cumulative damage, never establishing a credible threat to Red's objective.

## Lesson

Attrition is a numbers game—larger forces always win given enough time. When outnumbered, you cannot afford a grinding war. Your only window is to establish an immediate, credible breakthrough threat at the enemy's objective before cumulative damage renders your remaining units helpless.

Blue should have pushed both knights toward Red's fort from turn 10, arriving with ~18+ combined HP by turn 15. Red would have been forced to garrison defensively or risk a breach, neutralizing their numeric advantage. Instead, Blue spent 8 turns eliminating individual units, arriving at a 1v2 endgame with 4 HP on a single damaged knight—far too weak to threaten anything.

The counterintuitive principle: when outnumbered, *stop trying to equalize the board*. You cannot win the math. Instead, win by creating an objective threat *before* your attrition spiral completes. Unit-for-unit grinding is your worst strategy; forcing desperate defensive commitments is your only path. Push the objective, not the opponent's roster.
