## Situation

Turn 6: Blue's Archer could attack the weakened Red Mage (9/16 HP) from range with no counter-attack. Nearby, the Red Archer (13/18 HP) was harassing Blue's units. Red had two healthy Knights positioned to defend the center. The Red fort sat at (11,11).

Blue could have pushed Knights toward the fort immediately. Instead, Blue prioritized eliminating both ranged units over the next two turns.

## Lesson

**Ranged units are force multipliers that create impassable space. Eliminate them before pushing to the objective.**

Why this matters: A ranged unit deals damage from outside melee range, allowing it to punish any advance without accepting counter-damage. One archer can stall an entire melee army. Blue recognized this and made it priority #1.

Once the Mage (turn 8) and Archer (turn 7) were dead:
- Blue's Knight 2 advanced unopposed toward the fort
- Red's two healthy Knights, despite their full HP, never reached the fort in time (they were out of position)
- Turn 13: Knight 2 seized the fort and won

If Blue had ignored ranged support to rush Knights toward the objective instead, it would have been pinned by constant ranged harassment, unable to make progress. **Ranged units are the gatekeepers of approach routes.**

Conversely, Red's Knights—despite being alive and healthy at game end—were irrelevant because they couldn't defend a fort that Blue had already seized. The outcome was decided when ranged support fell, not by melee unit count.

**Principle:** Before committing your units to an objective push, identify and eliminate all enemy ranged threats. The tempo cost of this detour is always cheaper than the infinite stall of ranged units controlling your approach path.
