## Situation

On **turn 1**, Red immediately positioned 6 units in a forward convergence toward Blue's VIP (Tang Monk), with multiple units establishing overlapping threat zones around the objective. Spider Spirit moved within Sun Wukong's range, Skeleton positioned to threaten the VIP directly, and White Bone Demon moved to dominate the central corridor. Red made no effort to hold defensive terrain or engage in symmetric combat—all movement served the single objective of establishing pressure around Blue's VIP.

Blue responded reactively: Sun Wukong committed to killing the Spider Spirit (turn 1); Skeleton 1 was eliminated (turn 3); Tang Monk retreated but remained within Red's consolidating formation. By turn 4, White Bone Demon delivered the kill.

## Lesson

In VIP-elimination scenarios, **creating multiple simultaneous points of threat around the objective forces the opponent into reactive positioning and collapses their ability to defend symmetrically**. 

Red didn't engage in trades or fight for territory—they threaded converging pathways that made it impossible for Blue to defend the VIP without sacrificing units across multiple pressure points. Each Blue defensive action (killing Spider Spirit, repositioning Sun Wukong) left the VIP more isolated. 

The generalizable principle: **Asymmetric commitment to the VIP objective through coordinated forward positioning is superior to balanced/defensive play when elimination wins are available**. Rather than accepting even trades or neutral engagements, establish unavoidable threat density around the target and force your opponent to choose between protecting the VIP and preserving units. The first choice always creates a window; the second choice loses immediately.

In 4 turns, this focus delivered the game.
