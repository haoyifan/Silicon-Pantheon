## Situation

Blue's forces faced Red's army in a turn-based tactical elimination match. On Turn 1, Red initiated with Bull Demon's attack on Sun Wukong, dealing only 3 damage while taking 16 counter-damage. Rather than continue trading blows, Blue immediately eliminated the Bull Demon. Over the next two turns, Blue's heroes (Sun Wukong, Zhu Bajie, Sha Wujing) secured kill after kill: Skeleton 2 on Turn 1, both Spider Spirits and Skeleton 1 on Turn 2, the Demon King on Turn 3, and the final Skeleton on Turn 4.

Red's pattern was fundamentally different: spreading attacks without confirming kills. The Demon King attacked Sha Wujing twice without eliminating her. Skeletons chipped damage without finishing targets. By Turn 2, Red had secured zero kills while Blue had achieved four eliminations.

## Lesson

In elimination-based scenarios, **kills are the only metric that matters—not damage dealt**. Every unit you eliminate instantly improves your force ratio. Blue snowballed from 5v7 to 5v5 to 5v2 to victory in 6 turns because it focused firepower on isolated targets and converted fights into eliminations.

The critical principle: prioritize securing kills through coordinated firepower over spreading damage across multiple targets. Red's tactical error was attempting to position units and weaken the enemy without committing to elimination. This allowed Blue's tempo to accelerate—each turn, Blue killed more units than Red could replace, creating an unstoppable momentum.

In games with limited turns and elimination victory conditions, force ratio changes instantly with each kill. One focused attack that finishes an enemy is worth more than three attacks that merely damage. Winners don't trade blows—they eliminate threats.
