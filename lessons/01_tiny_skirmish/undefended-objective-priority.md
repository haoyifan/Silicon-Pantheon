## Situation

After Turn 2, Blue had coordinated focus fire to bring Red's Archer down to 7 HP—nearly defeated. On Turn 3, Red's damaged units repositioned defensively, but left their fort at (5,5) undefended and accessible. Blue's Archer, positioned at (4,2), could reach the enemy fort in a single move.

## Lesson

When an undefended objective lies on an accessible path, seizing it delivers faster victory than pursuing weakened enemies. Blue pivoted from **damage optimization** (finishing the 7-HP Archer) to **win-condition execution** (capturing the fort) and won on Turn 3 instead of grinding through additional turns of combat.

The principle: Assess whether a direct path to victory exists before committing further resources to intermediate targets. A fort capture that takes one turn is always superior to a multi-turn skirmish against a nearly-defeated opponent. Weak enemies are irrelevant if the objective is undefended and within reach.

For future players: **Map the path to your win condition early.** If seizing the enemy fort requires fewer actions than eliminating their units, and that path is clear, commit to it immediately. Enemy strength is tactical noise when victory is one move away.
